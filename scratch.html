🇹🇭 About Thailand

Thailand, officially the Kingdom of Thailand (formerly Siam), is a country in Southeast Asia with about 70 million people. I spent two incredible years living in Bangkok — one of the world's most vibrant and dynamic cities.

Thailand has never been colonized by a European power — a source of enormous national pride. Its name literally means "Land of the Free." The country is predominantly Buddhist, and golden temple spires define the skyline of virtually every city and town.

Top Reasons to Visit Thailand

  1. Culture & History
    • The Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew — the country's most sacred Buddhist temple
    • Ancient ruins at Ayutthaya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
    • Hundreds of active Buddhist temples (wats) throughout the country
  2. Food
    • Pad Thai, Tom Yum soup, and Green Curry — world-famous dishes
    • Mango sticky rice: simple, perfect dessert
    • Night markets open late with dozens of street food vendors
    • Regional cuisine changes dramatically — north, central, and south
  3. Nature & Beaches
    • Tropical islands: Koh Samui, Phuket, Koh Lipe, and many more
    • Ethical elephant sanctuaries in Chiang Mai
    • National parks with waterfalls, jungle treks, and wildlife
  4. Language & People
    • Thai is a tonal language with 5 tones and its own unique script
    • The Thai alphabet has 44 consonants and 15 vowel symbols
    • Thai people are known for warmth and hospitality — the "Land of Smiles" is no exaggeration

⭐ My Personal Highlights

Living in Bangkok for two years gave me experiences that no tourist trip could offer. Here are some of the moments I treasure most:

  • 🌊 Coordinated a 50-person flood relief team in Northern Thailand during the 2024 floods
  • 🗣 Became fluent in Thai — one of the harder languages for English speakers to master
  • 🍽 Ate street pad thai almost every day for two years and never got tired of it
  • 🚌 Navigated Bangkok's tuk-tuks, longtail boats, and BTS skytrain like a local
  • 🏙 Visited the ancient capital of Ayutthaya and trekked through the north near Chiang Mai
  • 🍺 Participated in Songkran — the Thai New Year water festival
  • 📚 Designed and ran a language tutoring program that helped dozens of new volunteers
